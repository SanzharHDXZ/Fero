What is ferrofluid

A liquid that
behaves like a magnet

Ferrofluid is a colloidal system of nano-sized Fe₃O₄ particles stably dispersed in an oil base. Under an external magnetic field the liquid deforms, aligns into structures, and becomes a controllable sorbent.

⚗️

Ferrofluid composition

Sunflower oil + Fe₃O₄ (iron oxide) powder. Surfactants prevent nanoparticle agglomeration, maintaining a stable dispersion and preserving the magnetic response.

🧲

How it works

The hydrophobic PET surface adheres to the oil phase of the ferrofluid. Magnetic nanoparticles allow the entire system — with bound plastic — to be extracted by an external magnet.

🧪

Role of water pH

Water pH influences the electrostatic interaction between Fe₃O₄ nanoparticles and PET surfaces. Studies report removal efficiency above 91% under tested pH conditions (pH 6–8). Iron-oxide sorbents maintain >91% efficiency across a broad pH range of 4–9. pH is a key optimisation parameter.

The microplastic problem

Microplastics are officially defined as particles smaller than 5 mm. Rivers carry 1.15–2.41 million tonnes of plastic into oceans every year. Particles enter food chains through bioaccumulation. Recent studies have detected microplastics in Caspian Sea fish and water samples.

Contaminated water

PET microplastic (0.5–5 mm) is suspended in water. Particles stay afloat due to Archimedes' buoyant force: F_b = ρ·g·V

Physics of the process

Plastic buoyancy: PET density (1.38 g/cm³) is less than water at pH 4–5, so particles remain near the surface. Drag force: F_d = ½ · C_d · ρ · A · v²
